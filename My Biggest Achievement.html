Reaching New Heights, Literally.

"You'll never see the world by riding in a car."

Planes. Whew. I never thought in my 28 years of living, that I'd actually board one. I was always a huge car enthusiast...I'd drive to England if I could. But one day, right before my birthday, I had a thought. "Why not get over my fear?" I did the unthinkable when I clicked the purchase button. I bought a non-refundable round-trip ticket to Denver, CO.

Why Do People Have a Fear of Planes?

  • Fear of falling
  • A sense of no control
  • Turbulence and accidents

    • Overcoming my fear of flying was a transformative experience during my trip to Denver. As I boarded the plane, a mix of anxiety and determination filled me. I focused on my breathing and reminded myself of the excitement that awaited me in Denver. With each passing moment, I felt the tension in my body gradually melt away, replaced by a sense of empowerment. When the plane finally took off, I was surprised by the rush of exhilaration that replaced my fear. By the time we landed, I realized I had not only conquered my fear but also opened up a world of possibilities for future adventures.
